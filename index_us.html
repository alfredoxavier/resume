Researcher - Master's degree student

EMBRAPA Instrumentation
  • Development of the work at the Optics and Photonics Laboratory of Embrapa Instrumentation (São Carlos-SP), leading by Dr. Débora Marcondes Bastos Pereira Milori. The work titled: "Evaluation of soil carbon accumulation and CO2 emissions in livestock production systems". The objective of this work was to evaluate the management of the livestock production system in Brachiaria decumbens pastures. The main focus of the work was the evaluation of soil carbon dynamics, that is, sequestering of C-CO2 and the emission of greenhouse gases, through the implementation of good management practices. Many carbon stock (EC) calculation strategies and evaluation of the effect of the managements were considered. Soils from productive pasture systems were evaluated with a focus on soil organic matter using spectroscopic techniques such as Laser Induced Fluorescence Spectroscopy (LIFS) and Laser Induced Plasma Optical Emission Spectroscopy (LIBS) in the construction and validation of methods for elementary quantifications.

    This master's dissertation was the national winner of the 2016 Vale-Capes Science and Sustainability Award, as the best dissertation in the area of ​​Greenhouse Gases Reduction (GHG)
